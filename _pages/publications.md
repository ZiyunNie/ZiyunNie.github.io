**[Poster] Multi-Task Paradigms in Eye-Tracking: Enhancing Diagnostic Social Attention Pattern Detection in Autism Spectrum Disorder via Machine Learning**  
Presented at *INSAR 2026 Annual Meeting*, Prague.
[<a href="/files/posters/poster_INSAR2026_ML.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Poster PDF</a>]

- **Research Background & Objective**：Addressing the lack of standardized protocols and cross-task generalizability in eye-tracking-based autism screening, this study evaluates the diagnostic social attention patterns in autistic children.
- **Key Methodology**：Applied machine learning algorithms to eye-movement features, employing SHAP analysis to determine cross-task feature importance.
- **Key Findings**: 
	- Combined-task models demonstrated robust performance, with Facial Expression Recognition (FER) exhibiting the highest discriminative power.  
	- Facial-region gaze features consistently emerged as universal markers for autism, highlighting cross-dimensional consistency in social attention atypicalities. 
- **Role:**
    - Designed and conducted data analysis.
    - Spearheaded the logical construction and visualization of research results.

**[Poster] Adaptive Learning Challenges in Autism: Reduced Evidence Accumulation and Prior Belief Updating in Probabilistic Reversal Reward Learning**  
Presented at *INSAR 2026 Annual Meeting*, Prague. (Top-Rated Abstract)
[<a href="/files/posters/poster_INSAR2026_PRRL.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Poster PDF</a>]

- **Research Background & Objective**：This study investigates the cognitive mechanisms underlying adaptive behavior deficits in autistic children by analyzing their performance in volatile, socially-rich environments.
- **Key Methodology**：Integrated the Probabilistic Reversal Reward Learning (PRRL) paradigm with a Drift Diffusion Model (DDM) to decompose behavioral performance into interpretable cognitive parameters, including evidence accumulation efficiency (drift rate) and response thresholds.
- **Key Findings**:
	- Autistic children exhibited significantly lower drift rates and slower recovery after reversal compared to typically developing (TD) peers.  
	- Computational modeling suggests that these adaptive difficulties stem from reduced evidence accumulation efficiency and impaired prior belief updating, which correlate significantly with clinical symptom scores (e.g., ASSQ, AQ, CAST). 
- **Role:**
    - Designed and conducted data analysis.
    - Spearheaded the logical construction and visualization of research results.
